Computer Basics & Practical Soft Skills Workshops

Day 1: Foundations & Communication Scenarios

Objective:

Apply hardware, Outlook, etiquette, and ethics concepts in practical scenarios.

Format:

Groups of 3-4, scenario-based discussion, and group sharing.

Time:

1.5 Hours (60 mins group work, 30 mins discussion)

Scenario 1: The "Vague Email"

Topic: (Outlook & Professional Communication)

You receive the following email from a manager in another department you haven't met:

Subject: FW: status
hey,

need the update on the Al-Hamra pipeline inspection. have a call with operations at 3.

-B

Your Task:

As a group, draft a professional reply. Your reply should:

  • Be polite and maintain a professional tone.
  • Clarify what kind of update they need (e.g., "the full report," "just the pressure test results," "a one-line summary").
  • Give them a clear timeline for when you can provide the status (e.g., "I am looking into this now and can send you the pressure test results by 2:30 PM.").

Scenario 2: The "Conference Room Chaos"

Topic: (Hardware & Workplace Etiquette)

You are about to start the mandatory daily safety briefing for a new drilling operation in 10 minutes. Contractors and team members are waiting. You plug your laptop into the conference room's main monitor, and... nothing. The screen is black.

Your Task:

  • Hardware: What are the first three hardware troubleshooting steps you take? (e.g., "Check power cable," "Check HDMI/VGA cable," "Press `Windows + P` to change projection mode," etc.)
  • Etiquette: What do you do at the same time? (e.g., designate someone to call IT immediately, have the briefing slides ready on a colleague's laptop as a backup, decide if you can start without the slides.)

Scenario 3: The "Found USB Drive"

Topic: (Professional Ethics & Hardware)

You find a small USB flash drive on the floor in the control room near a workstation. There's no name on it. You know that plugging it into a computer is the fastest way to see who it belongs to.

Your Task:

  • Ethics: What is the risk of plugging it in (to your computer and to the plant's control network)?
  • Action: What is the correct professional and ethical action to take? (e.g., Do not plug it in. Give it to your shift supervisor or the IT/Security department.)

Scenario 4: The "Active Listening Test"

Topic: (Communication & Etiquette)

One person in your group will be the "Speaker" and one will be the "Listener."

  • Speaker's Job: Talk for 2 minutes straight about "What you did last weekend."
  • Listener's Job: You cannot just nod. You must use active listening phrases (e.g., "So what you're saying is...", "It sounds like you felt...", "Tell me more about...").
  • Group's Job: Observe the conversation. Did the listener actually listen, or just wait for their turn to talk? How did the active listening phrases change the conversation? Be ready to discuss.

Day 2: The 60-Minute Report Challenge

Objective:

Combine Excel, Word, and PowerPoint skills in a timed project. Tests Time Management.

Format:

Groups of 3-4, one computer. 60-minute challenge to produce a report and slide.

Time:

1.5 Hours (60 mins challenge, 30 mins presentations)

Your Task

You've just been given a raw data export of recent maintenance work. A manager needs a "quick summary" for the weekly operations meeting in one hour.

Your group must produce:

  • Excel: A simple spreadsheet that is sorted, filtered, and calculates the Total Hours Spent. You must also create one simple bar chart showing the "Total Hours by Task_Type."
  • Word: A one-paragraph summary that explains the data (e.g., "This data shows maintenance hours for last week's work... The total hours were X. As the chart shows, 'Corrective Maintenance' is taking up the most time...").
  • PowerPoint: A single, clean, professional slide that includes a clear title, your summary, and your chart.
  • Time Management: You must plan as a group. Who is doing what? Who is the leader? You must finish in 60 minutes.

Day 3: Problem-Solving in Practice

Objective:

Apply the 4-step structured approach to problem-solving to realistic scenarios.

Format:

Groups of 3-4, assigned one case study, apply the 4-step process.

Time:

2 Hours (90 mins group work, 30 mins presentations)

Your Task

For your assigned case study, work as a group to apply the 4-step process. Use a whiteboard or large paper to document your answers.

  • Define the Real Problem: Identify the Symptom, the Real Problem, and the Root Cause (use the "5 Whys" technique).
  • Brainstorm Potential Solutions: List 3-5 potential solutions that address the root cause.
  • Evaluate and Choose a Solution: Pick the best solution. Justify your choice (e.g., "This is a low-effort, high-impact 'Quick Win'").
  • Implement and Review: Describe how you would implement this fix and how you would check later to see if it actually worked.

Be prepared to present your findings to the class.

Case Study 1: The Meeting Room Mix-Up

  • Symptom: You and your team show up for the critical Pre-Startup Safety Review in Conference Room B, but another team is already in there.
  • Problem: They also booked Room B. This is the second time this has happened this week, and it's delaying a critical safety sign-off.
  • Task: Find the root cause and propose a solution.

Case Study 2: The Confusing Email Chain

  • Symptom: A pipeline repair project is stalled because no one is sure who is supposed to approve the "hot-work" permit.
  • Problem: You find a 30-message email chain with 10 different people (from Operations, Safety, and Maintenance) all asking "Who is handling this?"
  • Task: Find the root cause and propose a solution to fix this project (and prevent it from happening on the *next* project).

Case Study 3: The PPE Cabinet

  • Symptom: The PPE (Personal Protective Equipment) cabinet at the site entrance is constantly empty of hard hats and safety glasses by 10 AM.
  • Problem: Crews are having to walk back to the main warehouse to get gear, delaying work. This morning, a visitor almost entered the site without glasses because the cabinet was empty.
  • Task: Find the root cause and propose a solution.
